Sights: Museums | Outdoors

Museums

New York is full of art. There are small art galleries and then there are the big museums that house thousands of pieces of art. My favorites are:

The Metropolitan Museum of Art is one of New York's biggest museums, spanning 5,000 years of art. There are a wide range of pieces from different countries and different times so anyone can enjoy but if you want to enjoy everything, plan out an entire day. Some of the notable pieces are: "Portrait of a Young Woman" by Johannes Vermeer, "Self-Portrait with a Straw Hat" by Vincent van Gogh, and "Washington Crossing the Delaware " by Emanuel Leutze.

The Museum of Modern Art is, as the name may suggest, a museum of over 200,000 works of modern and contemporary art. My favorite pieces are: "The Starry Night" by Vincent van Gogh, "The Persistence of Memory" by Salvador Dali, and "Drowning Girl" by Roy Lichtenstein.

If you like modern art and also enjoy architecture, check out the Guggenheim Museum, which is known for its dizzying design.

Outdoors

(All of these are open all the time, but the best time to see it is during the day with light, or sunset.)

Central Park may be one of New York's most famous landmarks, perhaps aside from Times Square. For someone seeking to enjoy nature by a walk or a jog or even a bike ride, be sure to check out the park. You can see from the map below that there are multiple entrances to this huge park. One is right by The Metropolitan Museum. It's always full of people so you will always be able to people watch!

The Highline is an abandoned train track turned into a park along the Hudson. It's full of greenery and its distance allows you to see many different views of New York City, from the skyline to an up close examination of some buildings.
